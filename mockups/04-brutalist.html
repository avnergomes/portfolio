I.

The work isn't the model.

It's whether the person reading the report can defend the decision it led them to. Methodology stated. Assumptions named. Limitations called out — at the top, not buried in the appendix.

That posture is the whole job. The tools are negotiable.

II.

Honest numbers, or no number.

I don't invent metrics to make a slide land. If the data doesn't support the claim, the claim gets cut — or the number gets a labelled placeholder and a plan to collect it.

III.

Built to be handed over.

Pipelines documented, dashboards understood by the analyst who inherits them, models reproducible six months out. The deliverable isn't the artefact — it's the team's ability to keep running it without me.
