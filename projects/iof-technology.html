IOF Technology Framework - One Amazon

Comprehensive geospatial data framework for environmental monitoring, carbon verification, and biodiversity tracking across Amazon forest ecosystems.

Geospatial
2025 - 2026
Panama / Amazon Basin

Project Overview

The Internet of Forests (IoF) is a comprehensive geospatial data framework designed to monitor, report, and verify (MRV) environmental conditions across forest ecosystems. The project integrates multiple satellite data sources with blockchain-based provenance for EUDR compliance and carbon credit verification.

Working with One Amazon Foundation and AECOM, I developed the complete data ingestion pipeline architecture, processing 200+ geospatial layers from multiple sources and standardizing outputs to a hectare-grid model with uncertainty quantification.

Supporting 29 environmental KPIs across 6 thematic groups, enabling Nature Positive Framework indicators for conservation programs covering 100,000+ hectares.

Technical Approach

Data Integration Pipeline

  • WHISP (FAO/OpenForis) integration for 200+ geospatial variables via Google Earth Engine
  • CHIRPS precipitation data processing with zonal statistics calculation
  • WaPOR evapotranspiration and water productivity analysis
  • ESRI Land Cover time series for change detection (2017-2024)
  • Landsat NDVI deforestation detection with structural break analysis

KPI Framework

  • Forest & Land Cover: 9 indicators (extent, loss rate, fragmentation)
  • Pastures & Agriculture: 5 indicators (expansion, interface analysis)
  • Fire & Temperature: 6 indicators (hotspots, burned area, LST)
  • Water Resources: 5 indicators (precipitation, drought index)
  • Soil & Carbon: 3 indicators (SOC, biomass, total stock)
  • Biodiversity: Intactness index calculations

Blockchain Integration

Implemented provenance-by-design architecture using IPFS for storage and Ethereum smart contracts for immutable proof anchoring, ensuring EUDR compliance and carbon credit integrity.
